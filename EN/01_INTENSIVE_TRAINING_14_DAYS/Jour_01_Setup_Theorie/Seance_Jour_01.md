# Day 01: Fundamental Theory of E-Commerce & Digital Marketing (2025 Syllabus)

## 1. Web Architecture and Programming Languages
### The Front-End Trio Fundamentals
- **HTML5 (HyperText Markup Language v5)**: Markup language structuring content. It defines the "Skeleton" (Titles `<h1>`, Paragraphs `<p>`, Images `<img>`, Links `<a>`).
- **CSS3 (Cascading Style Sheets v3)**: Styling language. Manages aesthetics (Colors, Typographies, Flexbox/Grid Layouts) and **Responsive Design** via **Media Queries**.
- **JavaScript (ES6+)**: Client-side scripting language. Essential for interactivity (Dynamic cart, real-time validation, animations).

### Infrastructure and Protocols
- **URL (Uniform Resource Locator)**: Address of a resource. Composed of the **Protocol** (https), **Domain Name** (www.jersey-shop.ci), **Port** (443 by default), and **Path** (/product/elephants-jersey).
- **CMS (Content Management System)** vs **Framework**:
    - **CMS** (e.g., PrestaShop, WordPress): Ready to use, ideal for quickly launching a jersey shop without coding.
    - **Framework** (e.g., React, Laravel): Component library for more complex custom development.

## 2. Digital Marketing and Sales Strategies
### Digital vs. Traditional Marketing
| Characteristic | Digital Marketing | Traditional Marketing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mediums** | Social Media, SEO, Emails | TV, Radio, Newspapers, Posters |
| **Targeting** | Surgical (by interests, age, location) | Broad and imprecise |
| **Interactivity** | Two-way (comments, reviews) | One-way |
| **Cost & ROI** | Low cost / Real-time measurable | High cost / Hard to measure |

### E-Commerce Business Models
- **B2B (Business to Business)**: Sales between companies (e.g., A bank buying school supplies in bulk for its employees).
- **B2C (Business to Consumer)**: Direct sales to individuals (e.g., Fan buying their football jersey online).
- **Marketplace**: Third-party platform (e.g., Jumia, Amazon) where multiple sellers display their products.
- **Drop Shipping**: Model where the e-merchant sells a product they don't have in stock; the supplier ships directly to the final customer.

## 3. Data Storage and Persistence
- **LocalStorage**: HTML5 technology allowing data storage (key/value pairs) directly in the user's browser. 
    - **Advantage**: Data persists even if the tab is closed.
    - **BTS Use Case**: Saving the contents of a customer's cart visiting the school supplies shop so they find it later.

## Sample Exam Questions (Preparation 19.5/20)
1. **What is the difference between client-side and server-side language?** 
   *Answer: Client-side (JS) runs in the user's browser. Server-side (PHP) runs on the computer hosting the site before sending the result to the client.*
2. **List 3 advantages of digital marketing for an Ivorian SME.**
   *Answer: National/global visibility at low cost, precise measurement of return on investment, possibility of retargeting.*
3. **What is the purpose of the `<meta name="viewport" ...>` tag?**
   *Answer: It is essential for Responsive Design; it tells the browser to adapt the site's width to that of the mobile screen.*
